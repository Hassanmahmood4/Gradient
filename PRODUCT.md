# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

CS and engineering students who are comfortable with calculus and linear algebra and want to learn machine learning. They arrive curious but often intimidated by the gap between dense textbook math and what algorithms actually do. They think in code and systems, value rigor, and lose patience with hand-waving. Their context: studying on a laptop, often late, wanting concepts to *click* fast through interaction rather than passive reading.

## Product Purpose

A learning platform that teaches machine learning by making the math move. Instead of static equations, students manipulate live visualizations (drag data points, tune learning rates, watch decision boundaries form) and see cause and effect in real time. The landing site sells this promise: a marketing page that hooks visitors, demonstrates the product through a visual mockup, shows social proof, and converts. The app itself (built later) delivers the interactive lessons.

Success: a CS student lands, immediately understands "this is how I finally *get* gradient descent," and signs up.

## Brand Personality

Bold and energetic. Confident, vibrant, motion-forward. The interface should feel alive and exciting, making ML approachable without dumbing it down. Voice is sharp and direct, speaks to technically capable people as peers, never condescending. Three words: bold, kinetic, intelligent.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS slop: clich&eacute; blue-gradient heroes, identical icon-heading-text card grids, the big-number hero-metric template.
- Sterile corporate ed-tech that feels like compliance training.
- Childish or over-simplified "learn to code!" aesthetics that condescend to a technical audience.
- Static, textbook-like pages with no motion or interactivity (the opposite of the product's whole premise).

## Reference direction

Stripe / Framer lane: clean and polished but colorful and playful, with depth, layered scroll-driven animation, and refined motion. The craft should feel premium while the energy stays high.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** The product's entire value is visual and interactive, so the marketing must demonstrate motion and interactivity, never just describe them. Practice what you preach.
2. **Respect the audience.** These are technical students. Keep real math visible, be precise, and never condescend.
3. **Motion with meaning.** Every animation should reinforce a concept or guide attention, not decorate. Kinetic energy in service of understanding.
4. **Distinctive over safe.** Actively avoid the generic SaaS template. The look should be recognizable and committed, not a defaulted blue gradient.
5. **Earn the conversion.** Each section should move a curious visitor one step closer to "I need to try this."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Because the design is motion-heavy, honor `prefers-reduced-motion` everywhere (replace large animations with instant or gentle fades). Maintain sufficient color contrast for all text, and never rely on color alone to convey meaning in visualizations.
